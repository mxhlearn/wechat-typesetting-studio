# WeChat Viral Patterns

Use this file when the user wants the article to feel closer to current high-performing Chinese public-account content, especially for tutorials, practical explainers, and tech-facing guides.

Do not copy wording from any source. Borrow only structure, pacing, and promise design.

This file reflects observable patterns from current Chinese tech-media and public-account-style publishing surfaces such as:

- 少数派 front-page guide-style headlines and utility framing
- 极客公园 headline-plus-summary packaging for topic interest and first-screen clarity

## Core Rule

Do not interpret "viral" as noisy.

For this skill, "viral-compatible" means:

- the value is obvious fast
- the title has a concrete promise
- the opening earns the next scroll
- the structure makes sharing easy
- the article feels useful enough to save or forward

It does not mean:

- fake urgency
- title bait with weak body
- exaggerated emotional wording
- copywriting that sounds like an ad

## Title Families That Travel Well

Prefer one of these title families.

### 1. Direct Utility

Best for tutorials and setup guides.

Patterns:

- `Windows 下安装 X：从 A 到 B 的完整流程`
- `把 X 跑起来：从环境准备到最终验证`
- `第一次配置 X 不想踩坑：这篇直接带你跑通`

Use when the reader mainly wants to finish a task.

### 2. Problem To Result

Best when the user pain point is stronger than the tool name.

Patterns:

- `总在 X 这一步卡住？这样处理更稳`
- `为什么很多人装不好 X？问题通常出在这 3 处`
- `想把 X 一次配通，你真正要注意的是这些细节`

Use when friction, failure, or confusion is the real hook.

### 3. Guide With Scope Lock

Best when the article needs to promise boundaries clearly.

Patterns:

- `这篇只解决 3 件事：A、B、C`
- `不讲大而全，只带你完成 X`
- `如果你只想先把 X 跑通，可以直接照这篇做`

Use when the topic is broad but the article should feel manageable.

### 4. Question With Stakes

Best for explainers and commentary, not routine installation tutorials.

Patterns:

- `X 已经能做到这一步了，接下来真正的问题是什么？`
- `当大家都在谈 X，普通人最该关心的其实是什么？`

Use sparingly for tutorial articles.

## First-Screen Formula

The first screen should usually do 3 things:

1. tell the reader what this article helps them finish
2. reduce uncertainty
3. make the path feel shorter

Good tutorial opening formula:

- title
- one short summary or hook line
- one paragraph that states the task
- one paragraph that states the main friction or likely pitfall

Avoid opening with:

- tool history
- industry background
- generic "recently many people asked me"
- emotional warm-up that delays the task

## Structure Shapes That Share Well

### Tutorial / Guide Shape

Preferred order:

1. what this article helps you finish
2. what to prepare before starting
3. step-by-step main path
4. verification
5. common mistakes or pitfalls
6. one light closing next step

### Practical Explainer Shape

Preferred order:

1. current confusion or pain point
2. the key distinction the reader must understand
3. the working method or framework
4. what to do next

### Commentary / Insight Shape

Preferred order:

1. the surprising fact or tension
2. why this matters now
3. the real implication
4. the reader takeaway

## Packaging Moves That Feel Strong Without Feeling Fake

- lock the article to one usable promise
- make headings do real navigation work
- surface pitfalls before readers hit them
- add one concise "save this" style checklist only when it truly helps
- let the ending land softly instead of repeating the whole article

## Blank-Page Draft Rules

When the user gives no draft and says "write it directly":

- choose one title family before drafting
- choose one structure shape before drafting
- state narrow assumptions briefly
- do not ask broad planning questions unless the topic is genuinely under-specified
- if the topic is operational, default to a utility-first tutorial rather than a reflective essay

## Anti-Fake Constraints

Reject these patterns unless the user explicitly wants them:

- "保姆级" as lazy filler without actual instructional detail
- opening with repeated rhetorical questions
- stacking too many benefits before showing the method
- claiming "一篇搞定" when the article clearly does not cover the full path
- long emotional endings after a practical tutorial
